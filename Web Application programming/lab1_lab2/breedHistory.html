The Tonkinese came about because Siamese and Burmese fanatics wanted a cat with the same traits, but at a moderate level. The Tonkinese is active and playful but doesnâ€™t need extra stimulation to be happy. They like to chat and chirp at times but aren't as vocal as their Siamese mother. Even their stature is a perfect combination; the short and stocky Burmese contributes to their bulk, but the lanky Siamese gives them some height and agility.

The Tonkinese first arrived in the U.S. in the 1930s. The same female cat who founded the Burmese breed in the U.S., Wong Mau, contributed to the Tonkinese breed; some Tonkinese lines can be traced back to Wong Mau today.
